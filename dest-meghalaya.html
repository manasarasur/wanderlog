Mawlynnong (Meghalaya) Travel Guide

Quick Travel Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: June to November (Monsoons)
  • Stay Duration: 2 Days
  • Local Transport: Shared Sumos & Hiking
  • Difficulty: Moderate (Hikes & Steps)

Mawlynnong, a tiny village in the East Khasi Hills of Meghalaya, is famous for being declared the "Cleanest Village in Asia". It is managed as a collective community by the local Khasi tribe, showcasing bamboo dustbins, floral lanes, and organic farming. It is a gateway to the spectacular living root bridges of the region.

How to Reach Mawlynnong

By Flight: The nearest airport is Umroi Airport in Shillong (90 km away) or Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati (170 km away) in Assam.

By Shared Sumo: Shared passenger jeeps (Sumos) run daily from Bara Bazaar in Shillong directly to Mawlynnong or nearby Dawki.

Key Experiences

  • Living Root Bridges: Hike down to Riwai village to see the single-decker living root bridge. Constructed by local Khasi elders, these bridges are grown by training the roots of *Ficus elastica* trees across rivers over decades.
  • Dawki River & Umngot: Take a short detour to Dawki to ride a wooden boat on the crystal-clear waters of the Umngot River, which looks like floating on glass.
  • Sky View Tower: Climb a 85-foot high bamboo tower constructing a panoramic view overlooking the plains of neighboring Bangladesh.
