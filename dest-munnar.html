Munnar Travel Guide

Quick Travel Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: September to May
  • Stay Duration: 3-4 Days
  • Local Transport: Local Jeep & Bus
  • Difficulty: Moderate (Hilly Terrains)

Munnar, perched 1,600 meters above sea level in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a historic hill station famous for its tea fields, forest reserves, and cool climate. Established as a summer retreat during the colonial era, its valleys remain carpeted in green.

How to Reach Munnar

By Flight: Cochin International Airport (COK) is the nearest airport, located 110 km from Munnar. You can hire a pre-paid taxi or board a KSRTC bus from the airport boundary.

By Train: The nearest railway station is Aluva (110 km) or Ernakulam Junction (120 km), connecting major South Indian cities.

Top Plantation & Forest Routes

  • Kolukkumalai Tea Estate: Home to the highest organic tea garden in the world, accessible only by a rugged 4x4 jeep ride. Best visited for sunrise views.
  • Lockhart Gap & Estate: Offers panoramic views of Bison Valley, historical tea museums, and plantation walks.
  • Eravikulam National Park: Sanctuary for the endangered Nilgiri Tahr (wild goat), offering walks through shola grassland blocks.
