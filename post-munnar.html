Chasing Mist in the Tea Gardens of Munnar

July 05, 2026 Clara Vance 14 comments

The journey to Munnar is a climb through shifting climates. We boarded the red-and-yellow KSRTC state transport bus from Kochi at 7:00 AM. For the first two hours, the route runs flat past suburban intersections and coconut groves. But as you pass the town of Kothamangalam, the road narrows, and the bus begins to labor. The air grows cool, carrying the scent of pine needle columns and damp earth. Winding around hairpin turns, waterfalls cascade down limestone cliffs directly onto the asphalt. By noon, we were surrounded by a sea of green.

Munnar is synonymous with tea. The slopes are covered in manicured green tea bushes, looking like velvet waves draped over the mountains. We chose to stay in a small homestay in Suryanelli, a quiet village located twenty kilometers past the main town. Early the next morning, before the sun rose over the peaks, we walked out into the Lockhart Estate paths. The valleys were filled with a thick, white mist that drifted slowly between the rows of tea plants.

As the light broke, we met a group of local tea leaf harvesters. Carrying large plastic sacks and wearing heavy aprons, they walked down the steep slopes with practiced ease. Their fingers moved with incredible speed, snapping the tender 'two leaves and a bud' tips off the branches and tossing them over their shoulders. We spoke with Lakshmi, who has worked on the estate for fifteen years. She explained that tea picking is an art: pick too deep, and you damage the plant; pick too slow, and the leaf burns under the sun. Her shears made a rhythmic *snip-snip* sound against the wood.

We spent the afternoon sitting in a tiny wooden tea stall at the edge of the estate. The owner, a cheerful man named Joseph, served us hot cardamom tea brewed with fresh mountain milk. As we watched the fog roll back into the valley, engulfing the tea plantations once more, we felt the slow magic of Munnar. It is a place where nature's rhythm determines the day, and where a warm cup of local tea is all you need to feel at home.
