Gokarna Travel Guide

Quick Travel Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: November to February
  • Stay Duration: 3 Days
  • Local Transport: Auto Rickshaw & Walking
  • Difficulty: Easy (Leisurely Hikes)

Gokarna, a small temple town in Uttara Kannada district of Karnataka, is famous for its beaches and pilgrimage sites. While the town center is crowded with pilgrims, the surrounding beaches offer rocky headlands, quiet shacks, and walking trails.

How to Reach Gokarna

By Train: The nearest railway station is Gokarna Road (GOK), located 10 km from the town center. Major trains also stop at Kumta (30 km) or Ankola (25 km).

By Bus: Daily overnight sleeper buses run from Bangalore, Mangalore, and Goa directly to Gokarna bus stand.

Five Beaches Hiking Trail

Gokarna is famous for its cliff hike that connects its five primary beaches. The hike takes about 3-4 hours and covers:

  • Kudle Beach: A wide sandy beach popular for sunsets, yoga, and beachside cafes.
  • Om Beach: Named for its geological shape resembling the 'Om' symbol, Om Beach features water activities.
  • Half Moon Beach: Accessible only by foot or boat, this crescent-shaped beach offers quiet hammocks.
  • Paradise Beach: A secluded rocky beach popular for camping and calm sea water.
