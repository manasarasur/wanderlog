Varanasi Travel Guide

Quick Travel Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: Oct to Mar
  • Stay Duration: 3-4 Days
  • Local Transport: Rickshaw & Boat
  • Difficulty: Moderate (Crowded)

Varanasi, also known as Banaras or Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River in Uttar Pradesh, it serves as the spiritual heart of India. It is a city that can feel overwhelming at first glance, but staying still reveals a deep beauty.

How to Reach Varanasi

By Flight: Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport in Babatpur is located 24 km from the ghats, connecting to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore.

By Train: Varanasi Junction (BSB) and Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Junction (DDU) are major railway terminals with daily connections across India.

Recommended 3-Day Slow Itinerary

  • Day 1: Ghat Walks & Ganga Aarti. Spend your morning walking between Assi Ghat and Dashashwamedh Ghat. In the evening, watch the ritual Ganga Aarti prayer from a wooden boat.
  • Day 2: Narrow Alleys & Temples. Wake up early to explore the labyrinth of lanes behind Kedar Ghat. Visit Kashi Vishwanath temple and try local Banarasi lassi in clay cups.
  • Day 3: Sarnath Excursion. Take a short auto-rickshaw ride to Sarnath, where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon. Explore the Buddhist ruins and park.
