Behind the Lens

I'm Clara Vance.

I started Wanderlog as a series of notes on my phone. In 2021, after spending seven years working standard office shifts in marketing in Bangalore, I realized that I knew subway lines and office parks better than India's scenic landscapes.

I packed my life into a travel backpack, bought a train ticket to Varanasi, and started exploring off-route. Wanderlog is my space to document rural communities, local cuisines, and travel guides.

Why I Travel

Slow travel is about listening. It is about staying in a mountain community long enough that the local shopkeeper starts recognizing your face, or sitting by the Ganges in Varanasi for a whole afternoon without checking your phone once.

Wanderlog isn't here to tell you where to spend your money on hotels or how to check off tourist lists. It is here to share the small things: the mist rolling over Munnar tea gardens, the spices of Old Delhi, and the simple freedom of a detour.

"To travel is to discover that everyone is wrong about other countries. It forces us to trade easy assumptions for actual conversations."

— Aldous Huxley
