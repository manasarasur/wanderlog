Coorg (Kodagu) Travel Guide

Quick Travel Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: October to May (or June-Aug for Monsoons)
  • Stay Duration: 3 Days
  • Local Transport: Rented Scooter / Auto
  • Difficulty: Easy (Misty headlands)

Coorg, historically known as Kodagu, is a mountainous district in south-western Karnataka. Surrounded by coffee estates and thick shola forests, it is often called the "Scotland of India". It is a land of rich tribal history, unique Kodava culture, and spice cultivation.

How to Reach Coorg

By Flight: The nearest airport is Kannur International Airport (CNN) in Kerala, located 90 km away. Alternatively, Bengaluru airport is located 270 km away.

By Train: The nearest railway junction is Mysore (MYS), located 120 km from Madikeri, with direct bus connections to Coorg.

Recommended Activities

  • Coffee Estate Walks: Book a homestay inside an active plantation. Walks expose you to coffee bushes, pepper vines, and cardamom plants.
  • Waterfalls Trail: Visit Abbey Falls or Iruppu Falls, especially during the heavy monsoon when they cascade down with tremendous force.
  • Bylakuppe Golden Temple: One of the largest Tibetan refugee settlements in India, located near Kushalnagar, featuring massive golden Buddha statues.
