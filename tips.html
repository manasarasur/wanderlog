Backpacking India: Travel Tips

We have spent five years navigating local trains, packing for monsoons, and exploring offbeat villages. Here are our essential guidelines to explore India safely and on a budget.

01

Minimalist Packing for India

Minimalist packing is not about self-deprivation—it is about avoiding the hassle of carrying heavy bags through busy train stations and stone alleys. In India, dress styles are conservative but light. Choose loose cotton garments, robust slip-on shoes for temple entry, and a light rain shell for sudden monsoons.

02

Budget Travel in India

India is one of the most budget-friendly destinations in the world, but it is easy to overspend on high-end hotels. Stick to local homestays, hostels, and local street food stalls. Travel in Sleeper or 3AC classes on trains to keep costs low and experience the country's local rhythm.

03

Safety Guidelines for Solo Backpackers

Safety is about situational awareness. When navigating busy markets or crowded stations, always keep your wallet in a secure front pocket or money belt. Buy a local SIM card immediately to stay connected to GPS and map routes. Only drink bottled or filtered water, and look for food stalls with a high turnover of local customers to ensure fresh dishes.

Always tell a family member or friend your travel plans, train numbers, and homestay locations before you head into remote valleys where mobile signals might be weak.

04

Train & Bus Booking Tips

Indian Railways is a massive network, and booking tickets can feel overwhelming. Register an account on the official IRCTC website well before your trip. Train bookings open 120 days in advance, and popular routes sell out within hours. If you miss out, look for Tatkal tickets (which open at 10:00 AM the day before departure) or use the Foreign Tourist Quota at booking offices in major stations.

For inter-city buses, platforms like RedBus or state government transport portals offer reliable sleeper bus bookings. Choose government-run buses (like KSRTC in Karnataka or HRTC in Himachal Pradesh) for reliable safety standards in mountainous regions.
