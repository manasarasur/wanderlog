Old Delhi Travel Guide

Quick Travel Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Stay Duration: 2 Days
  • Local Transport: Delhi Metro & Cycle Rickshaw
  • Difficulty: Hard (Intense Crowds)

Old Delhi, formerly Shahjahanabad, is the historic heart of the national capital. Founded by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639, it remains a bustling center of trade, Mughal architecture, and street food. Its narrow alleys hold histories and culinary traditions that have lasted for centuries.

How to Reach Old Delhi

By Metro: The Delhi Metro is the easiest way to reach Old Delhi. Board the Yellow Line and alight at Chandni Chowk Station, or the Violet Line and alight at Lal Quila (Red Fort) station.

By Train: Old Delhi Railway Station (DLI) and New Delhi Railway Station (NDLS) are located close to the historic boundaries.

Key Culinary Stops

  • Gali Paranthe Wali: A legendary alley containing series of small shops frying vegetable-filled flatbreads in massive cauldrons of ghee.
  • Khari Baoli Spice Market: Asia's largest wholesale spice market, offering spice mounds, saffron packs, and dry fruits.
  • Jama Masjid Alleys: The streets behind India's largest mosque host centuries-old shops selling Mughlai kebabs, biryani, and hot jalebis.
